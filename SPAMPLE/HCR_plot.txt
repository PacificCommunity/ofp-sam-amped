<h2>One-phase CPUE based HCR (HCR1, HCR2 and HCR3)</h2>
<p>The one-phase HCR is based on a simple linear relationship between relative CPUE and the resulting catch scaler. The catch scaler for each management cycle is based on the recent observed CPUE averaged over a specified period (<span class="math inline">\(Y_{ave}\)</span>), relative to a reference CPUE:</p>
<p><span class="math display">\[\begin{equation}
scaler=
g1*\frac{\overline{CPUE}}{CPUE_{ref}}+(1-g1) \\
\end{equation}\]</span> where <span class="math inline">\(\overline{CPUE}\)</span> is the average CPUE of the last <span class="math inline">\(Y_{ave}\)</span> years, <span class="math inline">\(CPUE_{ref}\)</span> is the reference CPUE, and <span class="math inline">\(g1\)</span> is a gain parameter that controls how reactive the HCR is to the average CPUE levels (Table~). There is also an optional parameter <span class="math inline">\(\Delta C_{max}\)</span> that limits the amount that the catch scalar can change compared to its previous value.The list of the parameters are listed in Table.1.</p>

<div id="two-phase-cpue-based-hcr-hcr4-hcr5-and-hcr6" class="section level2">
<h2>Two-phase CPUE based HCR (HCR4, HCR5 and HCR6)</h2>
<p>The two-phase HCR has two components, each returning an independent scaler for catch which are combined to return a single catch scaler.</p>
<p>Similar to the one-phase HCR, the scaler from the first component is determined from the ratio of <span class="math inline">\(\overline{CPUE}\)</span> to <span class="math inline">\(CPUE_{ref}\)</span>. The ratio is compared to two levels, <span class="math inline">\(Trig_{up}\)</span> and <span class="math inline">\(Trig_{down}\)</span>, that determine whether the resulting scalar will have a value of 1, <span class="math inline">\(S_{lower}\)</span> or <span class="math inline">\(S_{higher}\)</span>.</p>
<p>This first part of the rule has three outcomes: if <span class="math inline">\(\overline{CPUE}\)</span> is close to the <span class="math inline">\(CPUE_{ref}\)</span>, catches are unchanged, otherwise they are scaled up or down accordingly (Figure.2):</p>
<p><span class="math display">\[\begin{equation}
scaler.1=
  \begin{cases}
    Trig_{up} &lt; \frac{\overline{CPUE}}{CPUE_{ref}}, \quad S_{upper}\\
    Trig_{down} &lt; \frac{\overline{CPUE}}{CPUE_{ref}} &lt; Trig{up}, \quad 1.0 \\
    \frac{\overline{CPUE}}{CPUE_{ref}} &lt; Trig_{down}, \quad S_{lower}
  \end{cases}
\end{equation}\]</span></p>
<p>The second component calculates the gradient of a straight line fitted to the time series of CPUE for the last <span class="math inline">\(Y_{ave}\)</span> years (Figure.2). If CPUE is increasing the gradient of the line will be positive and the scaler will be greater than 1. Conversely, if CPUE is decreasing the gradient will be negative and the scaler will be less than 1. The scalar from the second component is calculated from the gradient and a gain parameter (<span class="math inline">\(g2\)</span>) (Figure.2):</p>
<p><span class="math display">\[\begin{equation}
scaler.2= 1 + g2 * gradient + (1-g2)
\end{equation}\]</span></p>
<p>The resulting overall catch scaler from the HCR is calculated as the product of scaler 1 and scaler 2 (i.e. the two scalers multipled together).</p>
<p><span class="math display">\[\begin{equation}
scaler=scaler.1 * scaler.2 
\end{equation}\]</span></p>
<p>The description of each parameter is given in Table.3. For the examples presented in this paper, an equal weighting was applied to each component of the HCR. A different weighting system can be discussed in the future.</p>
<p><img src="hcr2_1.png" style="width:30.0%" /> <img src="hcr2_2.png" style="width:30.0%" /> <img src="hcr2_3.png" style="width:30.0%" /></p>
<p><em>Figure 2. Two-phase harvest control rule: Phase 1 produces a simple scaler dependent on the relative CPUE ( <span class="math inline">\(\overline{CPUE}\)</span> to <span class="math inline">\(CPUE_{ref}\)</span>). Phase 2 produces a scaler dependent on the gradient of the recent trajectory of CPUE (greater than 1 for +ve gradients and less than 1 for -ve gradients). The resulting scaler for management of the fishery is the product of scaler 1 and scaler 2.</em></p>

<h2>Target rule mean length HCR (HCR7, HCR8 and HCR9)</h2>
<p>The catch scaler for the target-based mean length HCR is adjusted up or down or unchanged from the previous year based on the recent observed mean length in catch (<span class="math inline">\(\overline{L_{obs}}\)</span>) averaged over <span class="math inline">\(Y_{ave}\)</span> years (Figure.3). The target (<span class="math inline">\(L_{tar}\)</span>) and limit (<span class="math inline">\(L_{lim}\)</span>) represent the mean length in catch when stock depletion (<span class="math inline">\(SB/SB_{F=0}\)</span>) reaches the biomass target reference point (TRP) and the biomass limit reference point (LRP), as determined from deterministic projections, respectively. The catch scaler for the target-based mean length HCR is based on the recent observed mean length in catch (<span class="math inline">\(\overline{L_{obs}}\)</span>) averaged over <span class="math inline">\(Y_{ave}\)</span> years (Figure.3).</p>
